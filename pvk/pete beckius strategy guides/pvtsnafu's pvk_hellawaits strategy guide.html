PVK_Hellawaits Strategy Guide

Hellawaits is a large map that can be confusing to players the first time they see it. This will familiarize you with Hellawaits and provide some helpful information to form winning strategies.

PVK_Hellawaits

Objective:

Pilliage: The object of Hellawaits is to destroy. Simple enough, eh? The trick is to not let your stuff get destroyed before your enemies stuff gets destroyed. Your mission is to attack your enemies icon and bridge while at the same time preventing your enemy from doing the same. The last team with an icon or bridge left intact wins the round. To destroy the bridge, you must destroy its wooden support.

Your Targets:

Bridge - Destroy the Support

And the Icons

OVERVIEW:

The middle of the map consists of a three way bridge crossing over a pool of lava in a massive cavern. There is a ledge that runs along the outside of the cavern that can be used to traverse this area as well. At the foot of each section of the bridge is a sculpture that designates which team that tunnel leads to.

Pirates'

Vikings'
Knights'

Beyond each sculpture is a cavernous or cliffside area (the Pirates' area has no cliffside) that leads to the enterance to each team's base. There is a chain here that can be used to go on the upper or lower path. Beyond this point each team's base is essentially identical (one of them is flipped).

To enter into each team's base one must cross the bridge over the lava, into the antechamber or enter the cave under the bridge. Notice that if the bridge is destroyed the only way in or out of the base will be by way of the ledge that rings the lava below. There is a path carved from the rock that leads from the battlements into the respawn area. There is an upper pathway that circles from the battlements to above the other side of the bridge.

The antechamber has a high cieling with huge supports holding up the roof. There are two team specific doors to one side that lead in to and out of the respawn area. A hole in the other side of the wall leads into the caverns beneath the base. There is a ramp leading up into the great hall from here.

The respawn contains ammunition, armor, and food. There is a door leading from the spawning room, where a wall scrolls off basic information about the map, and another door leading up to the battlements.

The cavern beneath the base is filled with a pool of water. There is a cave leading to the front of the base, and a chain leading up to a way into the antechamber. In the water, there is a tunnel leading into a vertical shaft. In this shaft there is a metal button that resembles an up pointer and a grate. When the button is pressed, a door closes and water is pumped into the shaft. The water rises allowing enterance into the great hall.

The great hall holds the icon for each team up on a raised platform. Access to the platform is gained via two wide ramps on either side. There is a hole in the floor leading into the cavern and a covered archway leading out into the antechamber.

Tips and Strategy:

Communicate with your team members. Teamwork is essential to coordinating attack and defense.

When a bridge is out it is more difficult to go in or out of a base. If you destroy an enemy's bridge, it will be harder to destroy their icon. If the enemy has a strong defense, take out the icon first. If they focus more on attack, take out the bridge first.

The map is large. There will be longer times between attacks then on smaller maps. If you are a defender, and you have just beaten off an attack, head back to the respawn to heal and resupply.

Due to the ground attackers have to cover, make every attack run count. Don't engage in a battle unless you have to, and charge hard for your target.

The objectives DO NOT HEAL. Any damage done to them lasts for the entire round. Even if you don't succeed in destroying an objective, get a few whacks in.

Missle weapons and kegs are much less effective agains the objectives compared to melee weapons. Close in and destroy the objectives with hand to hand weapons. Save your ammunition for the defenders.

If one team gets both of its objectives destroyed it no longer has to worry about defense. Be aware that the full weight of their might will be out for revenge.

The default round time is 15 minutes. You can adjust the round time by altering the pvk_hellawaits.cfg file in the pvk root directory.
